# CREDITS

To the masters who taught me the art of the air, and to all those who encouraged and sustained my flight in every way, I owe my safety in the heavens and my desire to seek what is beyond the hills; as also the happiness I have found, the places I have looked upon, and the experiences with which I was blessed.

The example of **Kjartan Birgisson** gave me the image of the Tern: a lesson in grace of flight and simple representation. [[link](https://flickr.com/photos/kjartanb/14356696306/)]

As for the **creators of the Gruppo Font**, they taught me order and clarity in letters: that typography should be monospaced and clean, guiding the eye without distraction. [[link](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Gruppo)]

**Open-Meteo** let me into the knowledge of the winds and the atmosphere: to know where the thermal rises and when the storm gathers, served freely and without demand for keys or tribute. [[link](https://open-meteo.com/en/features#terms)]

I have to thank the **Norwegian Meteorological Institute** for their constancy and resilience: that when the primary voice falls silent, the pilot is not left in darkness, but finds guidance in their steadfast and public forecasts. [[link](https://api.met.no/)]

From **Paragliding Earth** I received the coordinates of the launch, which hold the collective memory of where pilots have stood before, looking down at the valleys. [[link](https://www.paraglidingearth.com/)]

This **kk7** taught me to see the patterns of the rising air: the thermal hotspots carved by thousands of flights, showing where the sky is alive. [[link](https://thermal.kk7.ch/)]

The **guardians of openAIP** recommended the invisible boundaries of the sky to me, and showed me the pattern of airspace that protects the flyer, built by the community for the safety of all. [[link](https://www.openaip.net/)]

To the **contributors of OpenStreetMap and OpenTopoMap** I am indebted for the contours of the earth: the paths of the mountains, the height of the ridges, and the names of the valleys, so that the pilot may never wander lost. [[link](https://www.openstreetmap.org/)] | [[link](https://opentopomap.org/)]

**Esri and its community** gave me the view from above: the silent sentinel of satellite imagery mapping the forests and landing fields as they truly exist. [[link](https://www.esri.com/)]

**OpenFreeMap** demonstrated generosity in hosting: the freedom to render the map in the Liberty style, cached for the pilot when the network vanishes. [[link](https://openfreemap.org/)]

It was the **builders of Meshtastic** that first showed me the power of the mesh: to speak through the air via LoRa when cellular towers are out of reach, binding comrades together in flight. [[link](https://meshtastic.org/)]

**GPX.swift** procured me the means to parse the paths of flights, turning XML tags into journeys. [[link](https://github.com/nikstar/GPX.swift)]

**Raphael Mor** let me into the true secret of compressing coordinates with his Polyline, saving precious space on the wire. [[link](https://github.com/raphaelmor/Polyline)]

I learned of the **authors of SwiftyJSON** the clean parsing of JSON, avoiding the traps of nesting. [[link](https://github.com/SwiftyJSON/SwiftyJSON)]

The **makers of osmdroid and MapLibre** brought the strength of native maps: the capacity to render grids and tiles smoothly on device, holding the earth in the palm of one's hand. [[link](https://github.com/osmdroid/osmdroid)] | [[link](https://github.com/maplibre/maplibre-native)]

I received the tool of the quick scan from the **ZXing project**: to pair devices through the camera lens without typing. [[link](https://github.com/zxing/zxing)]

**Google's FlatBuffers team** taught me the value of speed and memory-efficiency: to query vast regions of spatial data instantly without parsing overhead. [[link](https://github.com/google/flatbuffers)]
